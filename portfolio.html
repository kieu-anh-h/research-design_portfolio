2025–2026 · Master's Thesis · Tufts University

Exploring Fulfillment in MMORPGs

A mixed-methods study examining how World of Warcraft, Final Fantasy XIV, Guild Wars 2, and Elder Scrolls Online satisfy players' psychological needs for autonomy, competency, and relatedness. Used Self-Determination Theory as a framework, surveyed 158 players via the Ubisoft Perceived Experience Questionnaire, and conducted 18 semi-structured interviews. K-means clustering identified four distinct player profiles: Sociable Casual Players, Solo Explorers, Solo Role Players, and Sociable High Achievers.

2025 · Study Design

Avatar Customization & Avatar Identification

Designed a controlled lab study investigating how the gender and species of a virtual avatar affects similarity, embodied, and wishful identification — grounded in the Proteus Effect. 160 participants would inhabit a shared virtual environment with chat, group discussion, and racing activities, with behavioral tracking across time on task, emotion, and focus. Planned analysis: Multinomial Logistic Regression and One-way ANOVA with thematic analysis of group interview responses.

Experimental Design Proteus Effect N=160 Multinomial Logistic Regression One-Way ANOVA

2025 · Anthropology · Technologies of Enchantment

How Replika AI Commoditizes Companionship

An analysis of how Replika AI's onboarding flow, feature design, and in-app economy convert emotional need into financial commitment. Uses the Walk-Through Method and Cognitive Task Analysis to map how the platform builds user attachment through simulated intimacy, then monetizes it through paywalled customization and dark pattern purchasing mechanics. Draws on the CASA paradigm, the capture framework, and dark patterns taxonomy.
